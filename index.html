About

More About Me

The Geospatial environment is growing and maturing daily. It is a dynamic industry that keeps myself on my toes and forever learning the latest and greatest skills as well as overcoming the daily challenges I face. Problem solving can be done in a number of methods and this is one of the main attributes I like about this field of work. I am a person who does not like settling for the methods that work but rather improving these methods to achieve tasks in the quickest possible time. My various programming skills have led me to find new ways to look at the problems I face and achieve the most accurate results using various methods. I am a very extroverted, hard working person who enjoys sharing knowledge in teams. My many sports have helped me learn how to work in a team and achieve the best possible result. My goal is to further the field of GIS and through the many projects I will work on, adapt strategies that can be used to further the company I work for. The idea of generating the most accurate results for clients while leaving them with some extra information so they are desperate to use the company again is the key to my work.

This is me

I am a hard working individual who enjoys working in teams. I have my undergraduate degree in Geoinformatics from the University of Pretoria and am currently studying my Honours in Geoinformatics. My research focusing on “Modeling Cultural Interaction Between Known Robberg Technocomplex Archaeological Sites using a GIS Approach”.
